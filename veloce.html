🏎️ What Is Veloce at VIT Pune?

Team Veloce Racing (commonly referred to as Veloce) is the official student racing team and motorsport/Formula Student club of VIT Pune.
It brings together students passionate about automotive engineering and vehicle design to design, build, and race a formula-style
 car in national and international competitions.





🎯 Purpose & Focus

The club’s main goals are:

✔ Designing and manufacturing a Formula-style race car using real engineering processes.
✔ Competing in Formula Bharat, SUPRA SAE India, and similar motorsport events.
✔ Providing hands-on experience in automotive design, manufacturing, testing, and teamwork.
✔ Applying engineering theory to real-world challenges in vehicle performance, aerodynamics, and powertrain systems.





🚗 What Members Do

As part of Veloce, members typically:

🔹 Work on actual race car development — from concept to prototype.
🔹 Handle subsystems like chassis design, aerodynamics, suspension, electrical systems, and more.
🔹 Use CAD tools, simulation software, and fabrication techniques for construction and testing.
🔹 Participate in intense team planning, manufacturing schedules, and tests leading up to competitions.





🏆 Achievements

Team Veloce has built a strong reputation in national competitions:

✨ Winner in the Electric Vehicle category at Formula Bharat 2026 — including top finishes in acceleration, autocross, and overall
dynamic events.
✨ Multiple podium finishes and national rankings in SUPRA SAE INDIA and earlier Formula Bharat events.
✨ Consistent performance showing advanced automotive engineering capability among student teams across India.

—




👥 Who Can Join

Typically engineering students, especially those interested in mechanical, electrical, and automotive systems.

Members are often selected through club recruitment drives at the start of each semester. (Keep an eye on college mail or club 
announcements.)

Joining Veloce gives you practical engineering experience, collaboration on real machinery, and a chance to showcase skills on a
national stage — but it tends to be very time-intensive (lots of design work, fabrication, and testing).





🧠 Skills You Gain

Being in Veloce helps you build:

Engineering & technical design

Teamwork and project management

Problem-solving and iterative testing

Communication & presentation skills
(all valuable for technical careers and advanced studies)
